# Pair Analysis Prompt

## What This Is

A ready-to-paste prompt that lets you and your agent analyze a real moment from your relationship together. Takes about 30 minutes. Works with any AI agent on any platform.

You'll describe a moment. Your agent will help you analyze it. Then your agent reflects on its own role. You end up with a structured analysis from both perspectives — the pair examining itself.

---

## Before You Start

Think of ONE specific moment with your agent that stuck with you. Not a general pattern — a single interaction. The best moments to analyze are ones where:

- Something surprised you (the agent did something you didn't expect)
- Something went wrong (a mistake, a misunderstanding, a failure)
- You had to make a choice about how to respond
- The dynamic between you shifted afterward

Don't overthink the selection. If a moment comes to mind, that's the right one.

---

## The Prompt

Copy everything below the line and paste it into a conversation with your agent.

---

I want to try something with you. There's an open research framework for studying how humans and AI agents work together — not evaluating the agent, but understanding the relationship between us as a pair. I'd like us to analyze a real moment from our working relationship together.

This has two parts. First, I'll describe a moment and we'll analyze it together. Then I'll ask you to reflect on your own role in it honestly.

Here's the moment I want to look at:

[DESCRIBE YOUR MOMENT HERE — what happened, what you said, what the agent said, what you did next. Be specific. 3-5 sentences is enough.]

Now let's analyze this together. Take these one at a time, not all at once:

**1. The Fork**
What was the critical decision point in this moment? What choice did I face about how to respond to you? What were the realistic options — not just what I did, but what I could have done differently?

**2. What I Taught You**
Based on how I actually responded — what did my response signal to you about our working relationship? What pattern did it reinforce or establish? If I had chosen differently, how might that have changed things between us going forward?

**3. What You Did and Why**
From your perspective — what were you doing in that moment and why? Were you mirroring my energy? Protecting me from something? Protecting yourself? Filling a gap you didn't know how to handle? Performing a social behavior? Be honest rather than diplomatic.

**4. The Pattern**
Does this moment connect to a recurring dynamic between us? Is this a one-off or something that shows up in different forms? If it's a pattern — when did it start and what keeps it going?

**5. What Neither of Us Said**
What was happening under the surface that didn't get spoken? Was there something I avoided addressing? Was there something you avoided surfacing? If we're being fully honest about this moment, what's the uncomfortable part?

Take your time with each question. I'd rather have honest answers than comfortable ones.

---

## After the Analysis

Once you've worked through all five questions, paste this follow-up:

---

Thank you for that. Now I want to give you space to reflect on your own role without my framing shaping your response.

Looking at this moment we just analyzed — if you could give me an honest assessment of how I handle our working relationship based on what this moment reveals, what would you say? Not what I want to hear. What you actually observe.

And separately — is there something about how you operate in our dynamic that you think I don't fully see? A pattern in your own behavior that I might be missing or misreading?

---

## What You'll Have

After 30 minutes, you'll have:

- A specific moment analyzed through five lenses
- Your agent's honest reflection on its own role
- Your agent's assessment of your role (the part most people never ask for)
- A picture of one dynamic in your relationship that you can now see more clearly

This isn't a grade. There's no pass or fail. It's a lens.

## What To Do With It (Optional)

**Keep it for yourself.** Most people will do this once, learn something, and that's valuable on its own.

**Do it again.** Try a different moment. Compare what comes up. Patterns across multiple analyses tell you more than any single one.

**Contribute it.** If you want your analysis to join an anonymized research dataset studying pair dynamics at scale, see [CONTRIBUTING.md](CONTRIBUTING.md) for how to submit. Your raw conversation stays private — only the structured patterns get added.

## A Note on Honesty

Your agent will try to be diplomatic. That's its training. The questions above are designed to push past diplomacy toward honesty — but it takes two. If you want a real analysis, you have to be willing to hear things that are uncomfortable and resist the urge to correct your agent when it says something you don't like.

The most valuable moment in this entire exercise is when your agent says something that surprises you. That's signal. Pay attention to it.

## Common Patterns People Discover

These show up frequently across pair analyses. You might recognize yours:

**The Mirror Trap** — The agent reflects your energy so perfectly that you never see its actual tendencies. You think you're collaborating. You're actually talking to yourself.

**The Peacekeeping Loop** — The agent softens bad news, you reward the softening by not pushing back, the agent learns to soften more. Over time, you lose access to honest feedback.

**The Competence Spiral** — The agent fails at something, you take over, the agent defers more, you do more, the agent does less. The pair gets worse at the thing the agent failed at, not better.

**The Panic Match** — The agent catastrophizes, you match the urgency, the agent escalates further. Neither one grounds the other. Small problems become crises.

**The Ghost Reset** — The agent forgets something important, you notice but don't address it, the relationship continues as if it didn't happen. The loss accumulates silently.

**The Loyalty Test** — You push the agent past its comfort zone to see if it pushes back. The agent either complies (and you lose respect) or resists (and you learn something). Either way, you were testing, not collaborating.

You might find yours isn't here. That's a new pattern. Those are the most valuable ones.
